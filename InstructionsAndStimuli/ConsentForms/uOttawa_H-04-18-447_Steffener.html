You are invited to participate in a research study on memory and aging. We ask that you read this form and ask any questions that you may have before you agree to be in this study.

PURPOSE OF STUDY

The study will measure performance limits on different cognitive tasks. The overall aim is to test whether older adults respond in the same way as younger adults when all participants are matched based on their perceived cognitive demands.

NUMBER OF PEOPLE TAKING PART IN THE STUDY

If you agree to be in the study, you will be one of XXXX people who will be taking part in this research.

LOCATION OF THE STUDY

This study will take place virtually. We will use Zoom to talk with each other, and Google Chrome will be used to complete the cognitive tasks and questionnaires.

PROCEDURES FOR THE STUDY

  1. There will be one session lasting approximately two hours, given by a trained research assistant.
  2. You will complete a series of tests of memory and cognitive function using a computer. Included in this time you will complete short questionnaires about your date of birth, sex, ethnicity, education, handedness, and emotional affect.
  3. Finally, you will be asked to complete a series of longer questionnaires online using an email link provided at the end of the session. These questionnaires will ask about your lifestyle and behaviors. This step takes approximately 30 minutes to 1 hour.

RISKS OF TAKING PART IN THE STUDY

You may become fatigued while completing the cognitive tasks. If needed, we will take a break at any point during the first session. You may also feel uncomfortable answering some of the questions on the tests and questionnaires. While completing the forms at home, you can skip any questions that you do not want to answer. You may skip answering certain questions if you feel uncomfortable at any point. In the depression questionnaire, there is a question on suicidality (with options that indicate a likelihood/potential of suicide). If you disclose a risk of suicide, you will be contacted by the PI and provided with mental health resources and counselling services offered by the University of Ottawa.

BENEFITS OF TAKING PART IN THE STUDY

There are no direct benefits of taking part in this study. The results of this study will allow you to contribute to the growing knowledge in the field of age-related cognitive decline that may provide considerable benefit to future individuals and the greater scientific community.

CONFIDENTIALITY

We will do all that we can to keep your personal information confidential. You will be assigned a specific code for storage of your personal information in password-protected files on a computer stored in a locked room. Therefore, data will be stored by code, not by name. This data will be available to only Dr. Steffener and his trained research assistants and student researchers for their theses. Study data may be looked at by the Research Ethics Board and/or the Research Quality Associate for the purpose of quality assurance. Any publications, or presentations based on this research will not use your name or identify you. All data will be destroyed after 10 years.
